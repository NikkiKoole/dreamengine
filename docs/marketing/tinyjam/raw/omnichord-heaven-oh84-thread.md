## Comments

- **keyandcableco (OmniRepairShop):** "Seems really solid and responsive, I don't have a lot of
  criticism! … When you say you know how the chords and strumplate are musically structured — I'm
  interested, can you say more?"
- **omnichord-heaven (OP):** "Thankyou, this is a big help. **You are the only person to give any kind
  of response.**" … Both OH-27 and OH-84 are *models* using the early Omnichords' principle of
  operation (why they differ from other apps). It's heavy on low-spec devices, so OH-84 won't run on
  cheap tablets/phones. "My sound engine is copy protected using a kind of **DNA insertion** so if it
  appears on another app or website I will know." On musicality: Suzuki "made efforts to ensure they
  were musically *pleasing*, not music-theory perfect."
- **SabreSour (OmnichordContentHub):** explains the musicality — **(1)** the chord-section triads aren't
  true chords; they *fudge* notes (drop 3rds/5ths to make room for 7ths) so a triad sounds like the
  full chord; **(2)** the **F# note-wrap**: Omnis are limited to an F#-to-F# range, so the strum root
  isn't always the lowest pitch — they drop the top note an octave, so strumming bottom→top isn't always
  ascending (e.g. a B chord strums B1 D#1 F#0, B2 D#2 F#1, B3 D#3 F#2…).
- **OP (updates):** fixed strumplate clipping on two-finger input; swapped keyboard-mode voicing to the
  strumplate (had wrongly used chord voicing). The extended OH-84 is coming: custom skins, a "chord
  computer," MIDI (3 channels).

## Raw takeaways (see research-insights for the distilled version)

- Deep credibility + earnestness + the *exact* niche sub → **~10 up / 5 comments / "you're the only
  one who responded."** Credibility ≠ reach.
- The **authentic musicality** (fudged triads + the F# strum-wrap) is the real, ownable differentiator —
  "no other app includes" it. It's a product-quality bar omnichord people actually check.
- **Omnichord Heaven is a competitor** (building OH-84 desktop + OH-27 mobile, free, copy-protected
  engine), not a neutral resource — correction to our earlier channel notes. SabreSour
  (OmnichordContentHub) is the curator/tester, deeply embedded in the scene.
- Modelling is **CPU-heavy** (desktop-only OH-84; a separate lighter OH-27 for mobile) — relevant to our
  iOS/perf decisions.
