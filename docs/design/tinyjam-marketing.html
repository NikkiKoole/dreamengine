Go-to-market plan

One tribe per module.

Tinyjam isn't launched to "music app users." It's launched as a handful of small, precise campaigns — each aimed at a tribe that's already gathered, already nostalgic, and already priced out of the real instrument.

📍 ~20 enthusiast tribes mapped ✅ channels web-verified 2026-06-29 📄 source: docs/design/tinyjam-marketing.md

The core move

Sell to a tribe, not to a market.

🎯 The bullseye is small & reachable

"House producers" is millions of diffuse people. "The Omnichord Facebook group" is a few thousand who'll all see one good post.

✍️ The message writes itself

You don't pitch "a sequencer with IAP." You pitch "the Omnichord groove box you've wanted on your phone." The product is the headline.

💰 Price anchoring per tribe

OM-108 Omnichord ≈ $900. Rhodes MK8 ≈ thousands. A real TB-303 is a collector item. A $3–5 module reads as free.

🔁 It compounds

Each tribe that converts feeds the hub app and the one-time Master Pass, so the next tribe sees a product that's already alive.

Two kinds of tinyjam

…which map to two kinds of tribe. Lead with the instrument cults.

🎹 Instrument module → instrument cult

One beloved instrument, playable + sequenced.

omnichord · epiano · fm · moog · mellotron · stylophone

Tiny, intense, trivially findable (one FB group, one reissue hype cycle). The easiest, hottest first wins — lead here.

🎛️ Genre rack → scene tribe

A scene-in-a-box sequencer (the tinydaws rack table).

rebirth-house · dungeon-synth · exotica · radiophonic

Bigger but more diffuse — with passionate cores. The engineering pilot (rebirth-house) is here; the marketing pilot shouldn't be.

Verdict — the sleeper tinyjams hiding in plain sight

🛋️ lofi — the layup

Enormous audience already trained to one looping visual (Lofi Girl). Lowest-risk, highest-ceiling cart — and the engine doc rates it low.

🌆 italo + vapor — aesthetic goldmine

Synthwave is mainstream; vaporwave is the most shareable. Made for the pixel faceplate + 9:16 video — the content is the ad.

🪘 handpan — surprise sleeper

Not in the rack table at all, but explosive growth — hundreds of thousands in wellness/meditation/busker communities.

🪴 plantasia — the charmer

Viral 2019 reissue + cottagecore/houseplant crossover, and the cart already grows a houseplant as it plays. TikTok-native.

Build for love, not the register: gamelan, carlos, addis, tango, mariachi — the engine's proudest work; keep them free as the project's soul.

AUv3 is the multiplier. "I loaded three racks into AUM and jammed them together" is the Loopy-Pro-forum post that sells the Master Pass.

The sharing layer — URL-codec first

How forum people share their work. The recommendation:

✅ Now: lz-string URL codec

A song becomes a link — plays on click, no account, no upload, no server. The cheapest viral surface; fits ADR-0020 (no contribute-to-a-server). Already Tier-0.

+ IndexedDB

Local "my drafts" — work survives a refresh. Free, tier 2. Namespace the keys.

⏳ Supabase: later only

Only for a curated public gallery — heavier (moderation, spam, PII, idle-pause). Prefer a hand-curated "featured" list over open UGC.

Native generalizes for free: same blob → universal link / share sheet / tinyjam://; cross-device = CloudKit (free), not Supabase.

Video — the amplifier

Not the first move — the thing that makes the outreach land, then widens it.

Web-first: a video with no "play it free" link converts nothing, and you can't make a great clip until tribe feedback has made the cart great. But video isn't a later phase — it has three jobs at three moments:

⛽ Fuel

The first tribe post is far stronger with a clip. Woven into outreach from day one.

📣 Reach-widener

Short-form (TikTok/Reels/Shorts) reaches people who'll never read a forum. Forum = depth; video = breadth — each clip points back at the free demo.

🚀 Launch payload

At paid launch, video is the trailer and the reviewer fodder — Sound Test Room / Discchord review on video; the clip is the pitch.

The dreamengine edge. make-gif.js + compose-clips.js + the 9:16 Game-&-Watch dancer export mint marketing clips deterministically, from the cart itself — park a seed track in tools/clips/<cart>/, regenerate on demand. Most indies hand-capture; we generate. Audio capture works — clips carry their sound (the whole point for a sound-toy). One shareable, audio'd clip per module, repeatably.

Order: build cart → web demo + tribe outreach (rough clip) → learn & improve → polished short-form video → at launch, the trailer + reviewer pitch. Don't gate on a perfect video; do plan for a clip in the very first tribe post.

Launch sequencing

Engineering pilot ≠ marketing pilot. Learn the motion on the easiest targets first.
  1. Prove the funnel quietly — rebirth-house on the free web gallery; watch whether a rack holds strangers.
  2. First paid bullseye: the strum-harp (omnichord). Live OM-108 demand wave, a single findable target, an absurd price gap. Lowest-risk first campaign.
  3. FM box or tape-keys — evangelical instrument cults with built-in homes.
  4. First genre rack to market: dungeon synth (ancient-library) — a scene so tight it behaves like an instrument cult. Then acid (rebirth-classic).
  5. Roll the rest per tribe, each campaign nudging the Master Pass early-adopter price up a notch.

Plus a listening setup: where feedback arrives + how you judge "does it hold attention?" — the trigger for Stage 2.

The destination — a dedicated page, NOT the 400-cart gallery. Different funnel jobs: the gallery is broad discovery; a tribe campaign needs a focused surface — one cart, one URL, one story. Put a tinyjam hub on your own domain (tinyjam.<domain>/<module>) with a focused page per module. It's less work than the gallery — the web build already emits a self-contained cart.html/js/wasm bundle; a microsite is that bundle + a landing wrapper.

⚠ Host gotcha: runs on any static host (ScriptProcessor fallback), but for the better AudioWorklet audio you need COOP/COEP headers → Netlify / Cloudflare Pages / own server, not vanilla GitHub Pages. A quality lever, not a blocker.

Stage 2 — the App Store launch kit (later, gated on the trigger)

  • The native app — Phase-2 engine port + the spikes; module as IAP; hub-app shell; the AUv3 killer feature.
  • Store listing — original (non-trademark) name, subtitle, description, keywords, screenshots, preview video (the hero clip), icon.
  • IAP + pricing — modules + Master Pass; the launch-cheap early-adopter price (the Minecraft ladder).
  • Promo codes — ready to DM the helpers (the grandfather move).
  • TestFlight → posted to Loopy Pro for expert testers, pre-launch.
  • Reviewer outreach — TestFlight + clip to Sound Test Room / Discchord / Gavinski.
  • Launch posts — "it's live" to the wishlist + tribes + Loopy Pro, with the trailer.

How to show up

Gift first, then sell — without burning the tribe.

❌ The ask

"I'm building a music app — please beta-test it and tell me how to improve it."

Reads as unpaid labor + self-promo. The drive-by smell tribes punish.

✅ The gift

"I made a little playable strum-harp you can mess with in your browser — free, no signup — curious what an actual Omnichord head thinks."

Reads as one enthusiast sharing a toy.

Make the "help" invisible & optional

  • Share-your-song URLs (lz-string) are feedback and free marketing in one.
  • A/B toggle / bias knobs are the RLHF signal — they tune it just by playing. No chore.
  • Wishlist is a soft, no-paywall opt-in. "Help me improve" is a humble P.S., never the point.
  • The trap: feedback on the instrument = gold; recruiting them as a sales funnel = flamed.
The honest arc. The free web cart is a demo, decided as such from day one — not a free-forever thing you yank. And the trademark rule makes it cleaner: the free cart is a lovingly-named homage ("Omnichord-style toy"); the paid app is an original-named instrument ("Strumkit"). Not the same product behind a paywall — a free fan homage and a separate paid original.

If you pull or limit the free version — do it without betrayal

  • Signal the boundary early ("free demo — full app coming") so nobody feels rug-pulled.
  • Grandfather the early helpers — feedback-givers get the app free/cheap. How: App Store promo codes (official, free, ~100/version, expire ~4 wks) DM'd personally — they flip the same StoreKit unlock the IAP would. Discretionary, never a public "comment to win."
  • Keep shared song-links alive (a read-only player or redirect) — dead links read as "they bailed."
  • Prefer demo-limiting over removal — web = a try, app = own it; Module-of-the-Week is the soft version.
  • If you remove, frame it as "graduated to a real app," give notice, thank them.
🟩 Precedent: the Minecraft arc. Free browser "Classic" (2009) built the crowd — through the same TIGSource indie scene. Paid Alpha (2010) launched deliberately cheap (~half the eventual price), with a promise: buy now, the price rises as it nears release, and early buyers get every future update free forever. Beta raised it; 1.0 raised it again; by then, phenomenon.

Replicable structure: the free version stayed as a funnel · the climbing early-adopter price = the Master-Pass ladder · the cheapest price went to the early forum crowd who shaped it (rewarded, not used → that's why they evangelized) · Notch built in the open, shipping what the forum asked for.

⚠️ Caveat: Minecraft is an outlier (novel toy + perfect YouTube-LP timing). Borrow the structure, never bank on the outcome. "We'll be the next Minecraft" isn't a plan; "we'll treat early believers the way Minecraft did" is.

The one non-negotiable

Nothing Roland-named or Roland-skinned crosses the paywall.

Use the vibe, not the logotype: "acid box", "tape-keys", "strum-harp", "tape-choir". Body copy may say "inspired by the classic 1981 strum-harp"; the product name and trade dress may not. Loving homage stays free in the gallery; the paid app gets original names + original pixel faceplates.

This is not hypothetical. The very thing rebirth-house homages — Propellerhead's ReBirth RB-338 iPad app — was deleted by its own makers after Roland asserted an IP claim (they pulled it rather than fight). A beloved, successful product, gone over exactly this line. Homage free, paid faceplate original — full stop.
